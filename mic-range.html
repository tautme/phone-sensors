Mic Range

Live FFT spectrogram of what the phone's microphone hears, log-scaled from 20 Hz to Nyquist. Pair with the Hearing Range demo on a second phone to see what the speakers actually emit at each frequency.

Browser audio filters off. This page disables echo cancellation, noise suppression, and AGC on the mic stream. Without that, the browser would silently kill the very signals we want to see (low rumble, ultrasonics, narrow tones) before they reach the FFT.

Spectrogram scrolls top-to-bottom: newest data at the top. Frequency axis is logarithmic so low and high octaves get equal screen real estate. The response curve below the spectrogram tracks the highest magnitude seen at each frequency bin since the last reset; subtract the dashed baseline to see how much the mic actually picked up above its own noise floor.

Most phone mics roll off above ~16–18 kHz and below ~80 Hz, but cheap MEMS mics on flagship phones can surprise you in either direction. Sample rate is whatever the OS hands the browser, typically 44.1 or 48 kHz — Nyquist is half that.

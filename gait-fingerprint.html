Gait Fingerprint

Phone in your pocket or hand, walk for 30 seconds. Accelerometer peaks become steps; the timing, symmetry, and bounce pattern of those steps becomes a fingerprint that's surprisingly stable across walks — and surprisingly different between people.

Hold the phone consistently. Same orientation each time (e.g., always portrait in the front pocket of the same trouser leg). The fingerprint is the combination of you and how you carry the phone, so changing pockets changes the result.

Step detection: detrended acceleration magnitude, peak-pick above an adaptive threshold with a 250 ms refractory. Symmetry compares mean step interval on odd vs even strides (a smooth gait alternates evenly; a limp produces an even/odd gap). Spectral peak comes from a 1024-point FFT of the magnitude signal, indicating dominant step frequency. Bounce ratio is variance/mean of vertical accel — a heel-striker has a higher bounce than a smooth walker.

Saved fingerprints live in localStorage on this device only — nothing leaves the browser. Use "Forget saved" to wipe.
